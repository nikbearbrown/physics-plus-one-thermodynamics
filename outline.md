## Introduction

Thermodynamics as process physics — cycles, distributions, irreversibility. Statistical mechanics where useful (many particles → bulk behavior). The first law as bookkeeping, the second as the arrow of time.

## Chapters

1. **Temperature, Kinetic Theory, and the Gas Laws** — D3 Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution; temperature as mean KE per degree of freedom.
2. **Fluid Statics and Pressure** — D3 pressure visualizer; pressure as statistical-mechanics quantity, atmospheric and gauge pressure.
3. **Fluid Dynamics** — D3 Bernoulli + continuity; viscosity, Reynolds number, the unsolved turbulence problem.
4. **Heat and Heat Transfer** — D3 conduction/convection/radiation three-panel; heat vs temperature, Stefan-Boltzmann.
5. **Thermodynamics and the First Law** — D3 PV diagram animator; isothermal, adiabatic, isochoric, isobaric processes; work as area.
6. **The Second Law, Entropy, and Carnot** — D3 Carnot cycle + irreversibility demo; efficiency ceiling, Maxwell's demon.
7. **Statistical Mechanics and Entropy** *(NEW — to be written)* — D3 entropy demonstrator; S = k ln W; connection to information theory.
8. **Thermodynamics in the Real World** *(NEW — to be written)* — D3 heat engine efficiency explorer; refrigerators, heat pumps, life, climate.
